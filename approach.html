How I Teach

Your voice lives in your body. Let's start there.

Most vocal training focuses on hitting the right notes. But if your body is tense, disconnected, or shrinking—your voice will be too.

I teach singers to ground in their body first, using somatic exercises that help you feel safe, open, and present. One of my favorites: the fiddlehead fern unfurling—a gentle way to find aligned posture without forcing it.

When you're grounded in your body, your voice has room to be authentic. Not perfect. Not mimicking someone else. Just yours.

Performance isn't just about technique—it's about presence.

You can hit every note perfectly and still feel like you're not connecting with the audience (or yourself). That's because performance is about telling the story, not just singing the words.

I help performers:

  • Interpret lyrics emotionally (what is this song actually about for you?)
  • Find their stage presence (how do you want to be when you're up there?)
  • Ground themselves when nerves hit (breathwork, body awareness, anchoring)

I teach from two traditions: classical technique + Black American music.

I'm trained in bel canto (classical Italian vocal technique)—breath support, resonance, open throat. But I also grew up with soul, gospel, and jazz—music that's about feeling, not just precision.

I bring both into my teaching. You'll learn technique that supports your voice long-term, AND you'll learn how to bring soul, authenticity, and story into your performance.

No hierarchy. No competition. Just you and your voice.

I don't teach you to "beat" other singers or climb some imaginary ladder. I teach you to find your voice and use it the way you want to.

Whether you're brand new or already performing, whether you want to sing professionally or just for joy—you're welcome here.
